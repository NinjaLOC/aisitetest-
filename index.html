About CBD

CBD, or cannabidiol, is a natural compound found in the hemp plant. Unlike its cousin THC (tetrahydrocannabinol), CBD is non-psychoactive and does not cause the "high" commonly associated with marijuana. CBD oil is made by extracting CBD from the hemp plant, then diluting it with a carrier oil like coconut or hemp seed oil.

Health Benefits of CBD

CBD has been found to have a variety of potential health benefits, including:

  • Reducing pain and inflammation
  • Improving sleep
  • Lowering anxiety and depression
  • Reducing seizures and convulsions
  • Lowering the risk of certain types of cancers
  • Improving heart health

It is important to note that while research on the benefits of CBD is ongoing, more studies are needed to fully understand its effects and potential uses. Consult with your healthcare professional before taking any supplements or make changes to your medication regimen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CBD legal?

The legality of CBD varies by state and country. In the United States, hemp-derived CBD is legal at the federal level, but some states have restrictions on its use. It is important to check the laws in your area before using CBD.

Will CBD get me high?

No, CBD is non-psychoactive and does not cause the "high" commonly associated with marijuana.

How do I take CBD?

CBD can be taken in a variety of forms, including oil, capsules, and topical creams. It is important to follow the dosing instructions on the product you are using and consult with a healthcare professional before taking any supplements.
